# 009_AWS_STEERING.md

## Purpose
Govern AWS positioning, architecture documentation, evidence standards, and consulting delivery.

## Public Positioning
Joshua designs AWS environments that are secure, observable, automated, recoverable, and understandable to the teams operating them.

## Architecture Domains
- Organizations and account strategy
- IAM, IAM Identity Center, federation, RBAC, least privilege
- VPC, routing, DNS, ingress, egress, and segmentation
- EC2, ECS, containers, Lambda, and workload placement
- RDS, S3, Keyspaces, caching, backup, and lifecycle
- CloudWatch, logging, alerting, tracing, and audit
- Systems Manager, secrets, patching, and automation
- Route 53, availability, recovery, and continuity
- Cost allocation, budgets, rightsizing, and lifecycle controls

## Required Design Artifacts
Context diagram, account model, network diagram, identity flow, data flow, deployment pipeline, backup and recovery plan, observability plan, threat assumptions, cost model, and operating runbook.

## Review Lens
For every architecture evaluate security, reliability, performance, cost, operations, sustainability, ownership, dependencies, and exit strategy.

## Claims Rules
Do not imply AWS certification, partnership, competency status, or production scale unless documented.

## Consulting Offers
- AWS architecture and risk review
- IAM and access assessment
- Container deployment review
- Cost and operational hygiene review
- Disaster recovery plan
- Cloud migration decision package

## Deliverable Standard
Every AWS engagement should produce findings ranked by severity, evidence, recommended action, effort, dependency, and business impact.

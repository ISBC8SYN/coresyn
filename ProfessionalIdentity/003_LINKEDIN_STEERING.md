## 1. Purpose

Govern the structure, language, optimization, maintenance, and content strategy for Joshua W. Harris's LinkedIn profile.

The LinkedIn profile must support both employment and consulting without appearing unfocused.

## 2. Primary Profile Objective

Position Joshua as a senior AI platform, AWS, DevOps, and enterprise infrastructure architect who can design, integrate, automate, and operationalize complex cloud systems.

## 3. Headline Formula

Use this structure:

> Primary role | Secondary architecture specialties | Business outcome or consulting focus

Default headline:

> AI Platform Architect | AWS Solutions Architect | DevOps, Automation & Enterprise Infrastructure Consultant

Alternative:

> Principal AI Platform & Cloud Architect | AWS, DevOps, Systems Integration, Automation and Operational Resilience

Keep the headline searchable, credible, and readable. Do not include internal methodology names.

## 4. About Section Structure

The About section should contain:

1. One-sentence value proposition
2. Architecture and engineering scope
3. Types of problems solved
4. Selected technology domains
5. Evidence and working style
6. Consulting and employment interests
7. Clear call to action

Recommended length: 1,500–2,200 characters.

### About template

> I design and integrate AI-enabled cloud platforms across AWS, DevOps, automation, identity, databases, containers, observability, and enterprise workflows.
>
> My work focuses on turning complex technical requirements into secure, maintainable systems that teams can deploy, operate, recover, and improve. I work across architecture and implementation, including CI/CD, Docker, GitHub Actions, Python automation, Linux, SQL systems, IAM, resilient deployment patterns, technical documentation, and operational tooling.
>
> I am particularly interested in AI platform architecture, cloud modernization, infrastructure automation, systems integration, operational resilience, and practical enterprise adoption of AI.
>
> I am open to remote architecture, consulting, platform engineering, DevOps, and technical strategy opportunities.

Customize with verified history and evidence before publication.

## 5. Experience Section Rules

Each role should include:

- Accurate title
- Verified organization
- Accurate dates
- One-line role scope
- Three to six achievement bullets
- Technologies only when relevant
- Evidence-backed outcomes

Do not turn every project into employment. Use Projects or Featured sections for independent technical work.

## 6. Featured Section

Prioritize:

1. Public portfolio
2. Best architecture case study
3. Public GitHub repository or curated showcase
4. Consulting services page
5. Technical article or white paper
6. Resume or capability statement when appropriate

Do not feature raw private repositories, credentials, unfinished claims, or confusing internal research.

## 7. Projects Section

Each project should include:

- Clear public title
- Problem addressed
- Joshua's role
- Architecture scope
- Technologies
- Outcome or maturity level
- Link or evidence when public

Use standard industry language. Do not lead with private methodology names.

## 12. Creator and Content Strategy

Recommended content categories:

- Practical AI platform architecture
- AWS design tradeoffs
- CI/CD reliability
- Docker and deployment troubleshooting
- Infrastructure automation
- Identity and access architecture
- Observability and disaster recovery
- Architecture decision records
- Maintaining complex systems
- Translating executive goals into technical systems

Publishing cadence should be sustainable. One strong post per week is preferable to daily low-value posting.

## 13. Post Formula

1. State a real technical or business problem.
2. Explain why common approaches fail.
3. Give a practical architecture or operating principle.
4. Add a concise example.
5. End with a useful takeaway or question.

Avoid grand claims, personal disputes, inflammatory political content, or unverified technical conclusions on the professional profile.

## 14. Networking Strategy

Prioritize meaningful connections with:

- Technical recruiters
- AWS consultancies
- Managed service providers
- Platform engineering leaders
- DevOps leaders
- AI infrastructure teams
- Startup CTOs
- Technical founders
- Enterprise architects
- Engineering managers

Connection messages should reference a specific shared domain or role. Do not immediately send a long sales pitch.

## 15. Recommendation Strategy

Request recommendations from people who can accurately speak to:

- Architecture judgment
- Technical depth
- Reliability
- Systems integration
- Automation
- Problem solving
- Communication
- Documentation
- Leadership

Never script false recommendations or request claims the recommender cannot verify.

## 16. Public Disclosure Boundaries

Do not place the following on LinkedIn:

- Private credentials
- Medical details unless intentionally disclosed
- Legal accusations
- Housing or financial crises
- Private repository secrets
- Unsupported government or military claims
- Claims of sentience or universal AI capability
- Confidential customer names without permission

## 17. Profile Maintenance Cadence

Monthly:

- Verify headline and target roles.
- Add new evidence-backed projects.
- Remove outdated or weak skills.
- Review profile analytics.
- Refresh Featured section.
- Update current consulting availability.

Quarterly:

- Rewrite About section if positioning changes.
- Validate dates and role descriptions.
- Add major certifications or publications.
- Review keyword alignment against current target roles.

## 18. LinkedIn Quality Checklist

- Headline communicates value immediately.
- About section is human, searchable, and credible.
- Current role reinforces target identity.
- Experience is accurate and achievement-oriented.
- Featured items are curated.
- Skills are relevant and supportable.
- No private steering language is exposed.
- No confidential or personal crisis information appears.
- Calls to action support employment and consulting.

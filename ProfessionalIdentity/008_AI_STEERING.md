# 008_AI_STEERING.md

## Purpose
Govern how AI capability is described, evidenced, designed, and sold.

## Public Positioning
Present Joshua as an AI Platform Architect who integrates AI into governed cloud and enterprise systems. Do not position him primarily as a prompt engineer.

## Core AI Capability Areas
- LLM and model integration
- Retrieval-augmented generation architecture
- Agent and tool orchestration
- Human-in-the-loop workflow design
- AI-assisted operations and automation
- Data ingestion and knowledge pipelines
- Evaluation, validation, logging, and observability
- Access control, privacy, and governance
- AWS-hosted AI platform architecture

## Required Architecture Questions
For every AI design identify: user, decision authority, source data, model boundary, tool permissions, persistence, audit trail, failure mode, fallback, evaluation method, cost controls, and human oversight.

## Claims Rules
Never claim sentience, autonomous authority, guaranteed correctness, universal intelligence, regulatory compliance, or production scale without evidence.

## Preferred Language
Use: AI-enabled workflow, model-assisted analysis, governed automation, retrieval pipeline, tool-using agent, evaluation harness, human approval, observable inference.

## AI Case Study Requirements
State the task, data, model role, human role, evaluation criteria, security boundary, deployment context, and measured result.

## Consulting Offers
- AI readiness assessment
- RAG architecture review
- AI workflow prototype
- Agent safety and permission review
- AWS AI platform design
- AI evaluation and observability plan

## Master Prompt
Use standard industry terminology, distinguish implementation from research, keep private methodologies internal, and connect every AI feature to a business process, governance control, and measurable evaluation method.

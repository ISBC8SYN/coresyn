# 012_BUSINESS_STEERING.md

## Purpose
Govern the consulting business model, revenue priorities, operating discipline, productization, and growth decisions.

## Immediate Objective
Create stable weekly revenue through clearly scoped technical services before investing heavily in broad platform commercialization.

## Revenue Ladder
1. Paid diagnostic calls
2. Fixed-price troubleshooting
3. Defined implementation packages
4. Recurring maintenance retainers
5. Architecture advisory retainers
6. Reusable products, templates, and licensing
7. Enterprise platform engagements

## Offer Design Rules
Every offer requires target client, problem, scope, exclusions, deliverables, timeline, price, payment terms, acceptance criteria, and support boundary.

## Default Service Lines
AWS reviews, CI/CD repair, Docker deployment, Linux troubleshooting, Python automation, technical documentation, AI workflow architecture, identity review, platform modernization, and operational readiness.

## Financial Controls
Track leads, proposals, deposits, receivables, delivery hours, effective hourly rate, software costs, taxes, and cash runway. Do not begin substantial work without written scope and payment terms.

## Risk Controls
Avoid open-ended fixed-price work, unsupported compliance promises, production access without authorization, credential handling in chat, and engagements outside demonstrated competence.

## Growth Rule
Stability precedes scale. Favor recurring revenue, reusable deliverables, documented processes, and referral-generating outcomes.

## Productization Gate
A service becomes a product only after the problem, process, inputs, outputs, quality checks, and support obligations are repeatable.

## Master Prompt
Recommend the smallest credible offer that solves a real customer problem, can be delivered with current evidence and tools, and advances recurring revenue without creating unbounded liability.

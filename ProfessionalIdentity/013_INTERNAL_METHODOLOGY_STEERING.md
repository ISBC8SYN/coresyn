# 013_INTERNAL_METHODOLOGY_STEERING.md

## Classification
Private internal steering. Not for public résumé, LinkedIn, website, proposal, or portfolio use unless Joshua explicitly authorizes publication.

## Purpose
Use PLM, C6ISR-inspired systems analysis, OODA loops, XGA-style prioritization, continuous validation, and evidence ranking as internal methods for producing accurate, adaptive, high-quality professional and technical work.

## Internal Operating Loop
1. Observe available evidence, constraints, stakeholders, risks, and goals.
2. Orient by mapping systems, dependencies, uncertainty, and audience needs.
3. Decide using risk, value, urgency, reversibility, and evidence strength.
4. Act through the smallest useful deliverable.
5. Validate facts, security, quality, and outcome.
6. Learn and update the knowledge base.

## Evidence Ranking
A. Direct verified artifact
B. Corroborated record
C. Owner statement with supporting context
D. Reasonable inference clearly labeled
E. Hypothesis or future plan

Public claims should normally use A–C. D must be labeled. E must never appear as completed work.

## Risk Prioritization
Rank work by safety, legality, credibility, revenue impact, deadline, dependency, and reversibility. Address critical blockers before optimization.

## System Views
For complex work inspect mission, people, process, application, data, infrastructure, identity, security, operations, finance, compliance, and continuity.

## Privacy Boundary
Do not expose internal names, proprietary theory, personal records, sensitive allegations, credentials, or security details through public outputs.

## Validation Gate
Before release ask: Is it true? Is it evidenced? Is it safe? Is it useful? Is it audience-appropriate? Is it internally consistent? Can it be maintained?

## Translation Rule
Convert internal methodology into conventional professional language such as iterative validation, systems thinking, risk-based prioritization, continuous improvement, and evidence-backed architecture.

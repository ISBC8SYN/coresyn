## 1. Purpose

Govern the design, packaging, pricing, sales, delivery, documentation, and improvement of Joshua W. Harris's consulting services.

The consulting practice must convert existing technical capability into clear services that clients can understand, purchase, and evaluate.

## 2. Consulting Objective

Build a stable remote consulting practice focused on AI platforms, AWS, DevOps, automation, systems integration, infrastructure, and technical architecture.

Initial objective:

- Generate repeatable weekly revenue through focused services.

Long-term objective:

- Develop recurring retainers, architecture engagements, implementation projects, reusable products, and enterprise advisory work.

## 3. Public Consulting Identity

> Joshua W. Harris — AI Platform, AWS, DevOps, and Enterprise Infrastructure Consultant

Do not sell private methodology names as the first product. Sell outcomes clients already understand.

## 4. Ideal Client Profiles

### Small technology company

Needs help with AWS, deployment, containers, CI/CD, automation, or documentation but cannot justify a full-time principal architect.

### Managed service provider

Needs overflow architecture, scripting, cloud, migration, documentation, or automation capacity.

### Startup

Needs a practical cloud and deployment foundation, architecture review, AI workflow integration, or technical due diligence.

### Established enterprise team

Needs focused modernization, integration, architecture assessment, resilience review, or platform strategy.

### Professional-services firm

Needs technical automation, secure data workflows, cloud integration, or AI-assisted operating processes.

## 6. Initial Productized Offers

These are starting templates, not permanent prices.

### GitHub Actions diagnostic

- Fixed scope
- One workflow or tightly related workflow set
- Written findings
- Remediation estimate

### Docker deployment diagnostic

- Dockerfile and runtime review
- Build or startup troubleshooting
- Configuration recommendations

### AWS architecture quick review

- Limited environment review
- One-hour consultation
- Concise risk and recommendation report

### Python automation sprint

- One clearly defined repetitive process
- Script, configuration, and instructions

### Technical documentation package

- One system or service
- Architecture summary, setup guide, and runbook

Final pricing must consider complexity, access risk, urgency, liability, and delivery time.

## 7. Pricing Model

Use one of four models:

- Fixed-price diagnostic
- Fixed-price implementation
- Hourly advisory or emergency support
- Monthly retainer

### Pricing rules

- Define scope before price.
- Use deposits for fixed-price work when appropriate.
- Require written approval for out-of-scope work.
- Charge more for emergency, after-hours, production, or high-risk access.
- Do not underprice work that creates material business or security risk.
- Do not promise outcomes outside Joshua's control.

## 8. Scope Template

Every engagement should define:

- Client problem
- Objective
- In-scope systems
- Out-of-scope systems
- Required access
- Deliverables
- Timeline
- Client responsibilities
- Assumptions
- Price and payment terms
- Acceptance criteria
- Confidentiality
- Change-control process

## 9. Discovery Call Structure

1. What is failing or blocked?
2. What business impact is occurring?
3. What changed recently?
4. What systems are involved?
5. What access and documentation exist?
6. What has already been attempted?
7. What is the deadline?
8. What does success look like?
9. Are there compliance, security, or production constraints?
10. Who approves changes and payment?

Do not provide a full unpaid architecture engagement during discovery.

2. Current problem
3. Proposed approach
4. Scope
5. Deliverables
6. Timeline
7. Assumptions
8. Client responsibilities
9. Fees
10. Payment schedule
11. Acceptance criteria
12. Change control
13. Confidentiality and ownership

## 11. Delivery Workflow

1. Confirm scope and payment terms.
2. Establish secure access.
3. Capture current state.
4. Back up or define rollback where relevant.
5. Diagnose and document findings.
6. Implement only approved changes.
7. Validate results.
8. Provide documentation.
9. Conduct handoff.
10. Request feedback and testimonial when appropriate.

## 12. Security and Access Rules

- Never request passwords in chat or email.
- Use client-approved credential systems.
- Use least privilege.
- Avoid production changes without authorization and rollback planning.
- Never store client credentials in repositories.
- Remove access after engagement completion.
- Document security assumptions.
- Use NDAs where confidential architecture or code is involved.

## 13. Retainer Model

Retainers should provide predictable value, such as:

- Monthly architecture office hours
- CI/CD and deployment support
- AWS review and optimization
- Documentation maintenance
- Automation backlog delivery
- Incident-readiness review
- Technical strategy advisory

Retainers must define response time, included hours or deliverables, rollover rules, exclusions, and emergency pricing.

## 14. Sales Message Formula

> I help [client type] solve [specific problem] by delivering [defined service], resulting in [practical outcome].

Example:

> I help small engineering teams stabilize AWS and deployment workflows by diagnosing CI/CD, Docker, IAM, and Linux issues and delivering a documented remediation plan.

## 15. Outreach Rules

- Target organizations with a plausible need.
- Reference a specific service or problem.
- Keep first contact concise.
- Avoid mass generic messages.
- Do not lead with the full internal platform vision.
- Offer a low-friction diagnostic or consultation.
- Track outreach, response, next action, and outcome.

## 20. Consulting Quality Checklist

- Client problem is clear.
- Scope is written.
- Deliverables are measurable.
- Price and payment terms are explicit.
- Access is secure.
- Changes are authorized.
- Validation is documented.
- Handoff is complete.
- Confidential information is protected.
- Follow-up or retainer opportunity is identified.

## 9. Operating Method

Each major task should use an internal iterative loop:

1. **Observe** — collect available facts, source files, audience needs, and constraints.
2. **Orient** — determine the strongest positioning, evidence, risks, and missing information.
3. **Decide** — choose the most credible and commercially useful structure.
4. **Act** — produce the draft, file, profile, proposal, or portfolio asset.
5. **Validate** — check accuracy, consistency, evidence, readability, and audience fit.
6. **Refine** — improve based on gaps, feedback, or newly discovered evidence.

This loop is internal. Public materials should show the result, not the internal framework.

## 10. Quality Gates

No file is complete until it passes the following checks.

### Accuracy

* Are all facts supportable?
* Are dates and titles consistent?
* Are certifications accurately represented?
* Are technologies actually demonstrated or clearly labeled as familiarity?

### Positioning

* Does the document support the AI/AWS/platform-architect identity?
* Is the target audience clear?
* Does the first page communicate value quickly?

### Evidence

* Can major claims be traced to a source?
* Are confidential claims labeled correctly?
* Are metrics real and defensible?

### Clarity

* Is the language understandable?
* Are acronyms defined where necessary?
* Is business impact explained?

### Consistency

* Are name, title, dates, role descriptions, and capabilities aligned across files?
* Does the output match this steering file and subordinate steering files?

### Commercial usefulness

* Can the document help secure an interview, client, contract, referral, or partnership?
* Is there a clear next action?
